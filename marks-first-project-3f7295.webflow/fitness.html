FITBEEZ

FitBeez is an online fitness course booking application that allows user to discover fitness courses, studios, trainers and related fitness events.

Introduction

The Fitness, Health, Gym Club Industry skyrocketed as people start to care more about their  personal well-beings and quality of life.  studios and gyms offers a wide spectrum of courses like pilate, yoga, cross-fit etc. Seeing the trend,  we decide to do a conceptual design of a offline fitness platform that help people easily choose and take courses.

Challenge

No one really realized that the diversity of classes was a value, people tend to only stick to one kind of fitness activity.

Design Process Overview

Start Small, Move Fast and Learn by Testing

The process of this project is a perfect example of Start small, Move Fast and Learn by Testing. Given the scope of the project, it is not an easy task. After numerous internal scoping meetings, we decided to build MVP features first and slowly improve the product through testing. We make sure that we iterate as much as we can during the conceptual design and detailed design phase.

Literature Research

Understanding the trends in fitness and health industry.

We started off analyzing and understanding the current trend of fitness and health industry, In the upcoming years, wearable tech, body weight training, and high-intensity interval training (HIIT) will be huge, according to American College of Sports Medicine (ACSM). The 11th annual report surveyed 1,801 fitness professionals, including personal trainers, wellness coaches, exercise physiologists, and college professors.

Quantitive Research

Fitness market is growing unprecedentedly, more and more people start to work out regularly.

The Revenue of Fitness, Health and Gym Club Industry in United States in 2017 is 27.83 Billion Dollars and is predicted to increase to 31.53 billion in year 2021,
Active memberships at fitness center and Health club is 59.25 Million in 2017.
Average Percentage of US population engaged in sports and exerciser per day is 19% (20.2% Men, 17.8% Women)

Qualitative Research

Conducting interviews among fitness participants, trainers and owners.

We conducted 7 interviews among people that include active, regular and irregular fitness participants and another 4 interviews among fitness trainers and business owners to better understand their needs, we have discovered some key findings as below.

Personal and customized suggestions

People often don’t get personal suggestions, in a vast world of fitness courses, it is easy to get lost in choosing the fitness course that suit user’s need.

Hard to stay motivated for a long time

Most of the people can’t stay motivated for a long time,  which is the number one reason why people quit using online booking system.

Hard to keep track of progress

Keeping track of progress is crucial in staying motivated as people could see the improvement more obviously, however, more than 65% of the participants don't track their progress at all.

Too many booking platforms

Almost each studio or club has their own booking system, which makes it a hassle if users want to hop between studio/clubs.

Personal trainers don’t get enough exposure

Personal trainer is very popular right now, however most of the trainers reported that they don’t get enough exposure.

Small business organize free events to boost business

Most of the fitness business owner offers free courses and events to attract new users to register, they uses various kinds of platform to publicize their events. It becomes hard to manage over time.

Understanding the nuances between different user types

Combined with literature review, qualitative and quantitive research, we have categorized users into four major types, each of the user type have relatively distinctive expectations, needs and struggles. Then we created 4 data driven persona to assist our design process.

User Journey Mapping

Hard to discover new courses and staying motivated for long term seem to be a prevalent issue among users.

After talking to some regular fitness participants who have used several online course booking systems in the past, we have identified some of the frustrations in most of online booking experience. Most people find it hard to discover new courses, trainers and fitness events that suit their personal goals. User often can’t stay motivated for a very long time and usually don’t track their fitness statistics.

Literature Research

Finding ways to engage users and help them stay motivated.

Because a lot of the users can't stay motivated for very long, one key challenge for our design is to come up with a strategy to help user engage in the fitness activities and form healthy habits for regular exercise. Therefore we spent a considerable amount of time on figuring out how to motivate incentivize users.
I have summarized a few key findings:

01

Personal Intervention

Shift people's thinking from couch potato mentality to thinking like an athlete. This would require offering personalized suggestion and instruction.

02

Companion/Collaboration

People tend to have trouble asking for help, Yet in order to stick to a fitness program, we need buy-in and encouragement from other people support from other people trying to lose weight and maintain healthy eating and exercise routines.

03

Competition/Goal Setting

Competition doesn’t always mean two people, it could also be between you and yourself, Setting realistic goals that include clear milestone would create a ripple effect.

04

Monetary Reward

Sometimes, monetary reward work as a charm to help people stay motivated. This is very direct incentive for a lot of people as it is relatively straightforward and people could see the benefit right away.

Value Proposition

Converting a business problem to a design problem.

A good UX designer always understand the importance of the balance among user value, business value and technical constraint. To achieve minimum viable product goals, we did a value proposition assessment.  In order to make a fair judgement, we have involved many stakeholders to understand the constraints. From this, I have learned that, sometimes, making compromises is inevitable.

User Testing

We adopted participatory design principles where testing frequently occurs.

Usability testing is the primary way through which we get user input, through testing we are able to involve users at every step of the way.
