Overview

Problem Roulette is an online quiz application that aims to facilitate the learning experience for student at University of Michigan by allowing students to practice class material in the format of quiz. The project started in may 2018 and is still ongoing to this day.

Team 

The design team consist of one ux designer, one ux researchers and one data scientist.

My Role

Conducting user research,  prototyping and testing.

"Improve post-class learning experience” - Problem Roulette

View Project Detail

A building block to a complete online learning ecosystem.

Centralized platform to practice class-related material and prepare for exams.

There are around 2500 courses offered at University of Michigan and even more courses are added every year. imagine how much late night oil students would have to burn every year to study for various subjects.
‍
Seeing the benefit of IoT, the university has been devoted in developing in-house platforms and investing in existing services to improve students’ learning experience, Just to name a few, E-coach (digital learning instruction and guidance tool), Canvas (centralized academic management system),  Coursera for Michigan (Online courses of University of Michigan, offered via Coursera).

The university wants to design a new platforms that specifically target at course material practice and exam preparation to help student achieve better academic outcomes.
‍
This is how Problem Roulette was created - An online material practice tool that helps student with improving their academic performances through quizzes and practice exams, with a focus on STEM courses (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics)

Design Process

We have followed a very strict design process that ensures everything is iterated quickly and frequently to fail faster.

Problem Roulette has been running its service for many years and most of the solid ground work has been built,  however, the product merely just meets MVP goals to be a functional products and there are still many aspects that are missing from delivering a quality experience,  in order to make it fully conceived product,  we are in the loop of constantly getting feedback, iterating, validating with user to bring new features into life.

Design Thinking - Lean UX - Lean MVP Development

Heuristic Evaluation

A diamond in the rough

The current application is only delivering the minimum viable goals, which are still far from providing a seamless and  wholesome experience. We conducted a preliminary heuristic evaluation to identify some obvious problems, which aimed to inform our upcoming user research. Some of conspicuous issues are:

Inconsistent session set up

Before one could start a session, they would have to go through a set up process where users would have to choose subject, select the topics, set the duration and etc. As much as it is functional, the whole process feels inconsistent at places and is not ready to be scaled to accommodate more features in the future.

Hard to keep track of progress

People find it hard to systematically track their study progress under different topics, which prevents user from forming long term study habit and strategizing their study plan.

Users are Isolated

Users are isolated on the platform, they can’t see how their peers are doing in comparison to theirs. Besides, collaboration is not supported so that users can’t form a study group and work together.

Data Driven Research

We looked through the data and identified behavior patterns among different user groups

With the help of the data scientist on the team, we have put together a report about how new users and returning users are using the system. We analyzed the relation between grade, average daily usage, session duration, session abandonment rate.

Difficult Question vs Easy Question

Low achieving students tend to skip difficult question easier than high achieving student, Higher-achieving students are more willing to take on the challenge of spending longer time on difficult questions.

Grade and average time spend on each question

People that spend more time on difficult question tend to achieve better academic result and obtain higher grade. lower-achieving students tend to spend less time on difficult question or skip more often.

Tracking progress has positive impact on learning

People that constantly check their progress in learning tend to achieve better academic outcomes.
Knowing this correlation, we started to think about how to devise means that would encourage people to not only spend more time on difficult question but also be more aware of their progress and overall performance.

Qualitative User Research

Users are using alternative platforms to practice as Problem Roulette can't accommodate their growing needs.

We have conducted 8 interviews, of which the participants ranges from first year newly admitted students to third year under graduate students, the result is out of our surprise as we realized that students are using other platforms to practice quizzes as the current application are lacking a lot features to make it a enjoyable experience.

Group Study

One thing that got my attention is how student actually use PR, there are a considerable amount of student tend to form a study group and work on problems together,  However, PR was initially designed for individual practice.

Poor System Navigation

User feel confused as the platforms doesn’t align with their mental model. they often don’t know why they ended up on certain page.

Confusing Set Up Process

As mentioned in the earlier heuristic eval, most users also find the set up process confusing, Sometimes the set up process is ended without notifying users in advance, lack of indicator for progress and disorganized button ate the main reasons.

Personalized Experience

User can’t track their progress, and wish there was visualization that shows their daily activity and performance.

Poor Recoverability

Once the set up is abandoned due to various reason, user can’t recover from it, which result in frustration and therefore user abandonment  

Unguided Study Mode

Other than telling whether the selected answer is right or wrong, the study mode doesn’t provide any additional guidance if user get stuck

User Journey Mapping

We tried mapping out user’s journey to understand where problems occurs.

We formed a focus group that would report their experience of using PR regularly to help us understand where problem occurs. Many issues were discovered through mapping out the journey, which ultimately compromises experience as a whole.

For instance, during set up process, user could not go back to the module that they created previously and thus have to create a new one. Problem like this greatly affected how use judge the experience and therefore causes users to ultimately quit using Problem Roulette.

Literature Review

Conducted extensive literature research to understand how people study, how study habits are formed and what role practice plays in obtaining knowledge.

To create a learning experience where users would feel supported at all time requires us to delve deeper into how people actually study, We want to understand how people actually learn new knowledge and how practice and repetition plays in the overall learning experience. With the support of the human behavior scientists on the team, we spent an extensive amount of time doing literature research to understand this process.

We identified a set of factors that ultimately contribute to successful and efficient learning experience. What PR come to play is mostly under Study & Review where memory, progress, goals and repetition play significant roles in obtaining new knowledge efficiently,

Value Proposition

We did some exploration on the feature level

Knowing where problems occur and how users are using PR, we have brainstormed a lot of features that would be beneficial to the leaners.  As much as we like all of the features, we had to take into account of some of the constraints. We have prioritized some features over others based implementation difficulty and user value.

Interaction Flow Design

Building conceptual model for the set up process to reduce confusion and abandonment rate.

This is the area where most of the abandonment occurs due to frustration, we have built an interaction flow map that tries to encompass a variety of situation, we make sure intervention was implemented at places that cause user frustration,

Interaction Flow Design

Designing a guided learning experience.

To support leaners when they are stuck or about to give up, we have carefully devised a considerable amount of prevention and helping mechanism to ensure a seamless and guided learning experience.
Some of the high-level goals that we want to achieve are:

- Provide hints or clues to guide user to find answer on their own instead of showing them the correct answer directly.
- Reduce the frustration when user get stuck.
- Encourage user to spend more time on difficult questions.

Final Solution Showcase

The tool bar feature offers a number of widgets that would make the learning experience more effortless.

Often time when we study, we would use other tools to aid the learning experience. Same goes for online learning, having tools that would facilitate this experience would drastically improve efficiency. We implemented three widget to provide an experience that boost the efficiency of learning meanwhile maintaining a uninterrupted learning flow.

Calculator

A lot of the STEM (Science, technology, engineering and Mathematics) courses involve a lot of calculation, by providing a simple calculator, user could save the time from taking out their phone or using a physical calculator for simple mathematical computation.

Note

Taking note is proven to be an indispensable part of the learning process, now the note feature allows user to quickly type something for later review.

Messenger

PR uses web socket to allow people to communicate with others in the group quickly. Not only they could send text and image, they could also send question to other users on the platform.

Final Solution Showcase

User now could archive a question in notebook for later review.

Now when user encounter a difficult question, they could put it in the notebook and come back later to review it in detail. this feature would ensure that people would spend more time on solving difficult questions instead of skipping them all the time.

Final Solution Showcase

Improved exam mode allows user to create a customized exams that suit their learning objectives and traverse through questions easily.

We offered more flexibility in creating a exam that suit different student's level through machine learning, the algorithm that power the exam mode is very dynamic as it would generate the session with reasonable difficulties for a certain student during the set up process

In addition, during the exams mode, user could easily mark and traverse through questions to answer all the question strategically.

Final Solution Showcase

Progress and performance could be easily measured and accessed to allow learner to make plan accordingly.

As it is mentioned in the early literature research, one of the learning habits that has profound impact is to regularly track progress and reflect on the learning result, by providing visualization on how users perform overtime. With this feature, PR allows learner to be more aware of their learning outcome and progress.

Final Solution Showcase

Building a community where learners could motivate each others.

Now users could choose to publish their daily activity and share it with friends on the platform.  Besides, leaner could also see other people’s activities, which in return further motivate leaners. With a community full of active learners, students could stay motivated.
