Prologue

This case study showcase several projects that I have participated at Fountain for the past 18 months, which includes multiple facets of the hiring automation industry.

Project Overview

Fountain has multiple functional teams that tend to only focus on one aspect of the product. As much as it allows each team to dive deeply in their own corresponding area,  It results in each team only optimizing locally not globally. Overtime its drawback start to emerge.

INTRODUCTION

Fountain - Hiring Software for Gig Economy

Fountain is an Applicant Tracking System (ATS) that primarily focuses on gig economy and industres that have a need for high volume hiring.

Fountain was established in 2014 by our CEO Keith Ryu and is considered as one of the unicorns in the industry. “Champion the Underdog” has always been our mantra and has led us to overcome many challenges over the years.

We have assisted more than 80 Fortune 1000 companies, as well as plenty of small/medium companies, to scale their business through automating their hiring process.

Besides, around 3.5 millions of candidates landed their job offers through our platform.

Market Snapshot

High Volume, High Turnover Rate, High Cost

With the rise of gig economy like Uber, Cabify(Europe), Airbnb, a growing need for hourly/freelance workers has emerged. The gig economy is made up of three main components:  (i) the independent workers paid by the gig (i.e., a task or a project) as opposed to those workers who receive a salary or hourly wage;  (ii) the consumers who need a specific service, for example, a ride to their next destination, or a particular item delivered. and (iii) the companies that connect the worker to the consumer in a direct manner, including app-based technology platforms.

The characteristics of gig economy hiring are high volume, high turnover rate, and high hiring cost.

Fountain seized this opportunity and developed a platform that primarily focused on assisting gig business to hire efficiently and cheaply. Regarding the common pain points in gig talent acquisition, Fountain is centered around 3 major characteristics: automation, sourcing and streamlined process.

Business Trajectory

However, Fountain didn’t just stop there, we want to expand our business both vertically and horizontally to further strengthen our competitive edge. Vertically, we want to go upstream to include sourcing and worker marketplace in our service. Horizontally, we want to expand beyond just gig economy. As we grow, retail and hospitality come to our vision due their similarities with gig economy.

Problem Statement

Diverging "Swim Lanes"

Internally, as shown below, there are 6 teams that focus on different aspects of our product and they are relatively independent from each other. As much as each team has a clear boundary of responsibility and agenda, the teams start to diverge. overtime, it slowly causes the product to feel disjointed and inconsistent. Besides, due to lack communication, it is extremely hard to get aligned on the strategic level, causing the teams unable to collaborate and optimize on a deeper level.



Core Product/Screening - Optimize the applicant screening experience.
Sourcing Service - Help clients outsource more qualified workers to achieve hiring goal.
Worker Portal -  Optimize job application experience for workers.
Integration - Integrate with other 3rd party apps to streamline the hiring experience.
Workflow - Optimize the workflow setup process.
SMB - An simplified hiring/screening tool for small and medium businesses.

"We need to build one cohesive product"

As time goes by, the product experience suffered from each team operating separately. Some customers start to churn and decide not to renew their contracts, the reasons for this issue are;

1. The whole product experience is very disconnected
2. Since each team optimize mostly just locally not globally, the product quality varies drastically.
3. Not enough communication is done across teams
4. Research is done on a team level and sometimes leave blind spots
5. Visual style is not consistent due to lack of design system

Problem Solving Process

The following chart demonstrates my general problem solving process: Discovery, Define, Design and Validation. I started with collecting information both internally and externally to understand the entire problem landscape.

DISCOVERY - INTERNAL STAKEHOLDER

Product is generally good but customer churn risk starts to expose, worker experience is critical to success

In the beginning of the discovery phase, I organized an open discussion with the customer support team given that they work closely with clients on a daily basis and are very familiar with their common needs and struggles. Prior to this meeting, I joined 4 new customer video calls to observe how new customers are being on-boarded and where they usually get tripped over. This allows me to attend the meeting with some preconceived assumptions and a paper full of questions.

In the meeting,  we first went over some of the primary user types and talked about their responsibilities and common tasks within their organization, and then we discussed some of the recurring product issues with detailed examples. Last but not the least, we did a whiteboard session to draw all the clues together to form some arbitrary assumptions that need to be further validated.

Insight: understand the dynamics among our users

A hiring department usually consists of three functional units, each unit generally has very distinct responsibility area. Worker experience is at the center of of all units and therefore is considered the most critical factor of success. Understanding the dynamics among each unit gives us a holistic perspective on how team works together to make things work.

User Types

For enterprise company, the talent acquisition department is generally comprised of three major user types (Recruiter, Operation Manager, Director of Talent)

For small/medium business, due to lack of resource, it is usually one general manager that is handeling all hiring related tasks.

Worker Experience

Worker experience is critical to having higher conversion rate, so it is given the highest priority and is constantly iterated to ensure that it is a seamless experience.

Success Criteria

Conversion Rate>Operation Efficiency>Hiring Cycle

the major complaints are centered around unsatisfactory conversion rate, poor operation efficiency and functional bugs.

DISCOVERY - COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

Sourcing and applicant market place have become the new revenue pieces among ATS,  AI driven services are widely adopted

In order to stay ahead of the game and see the entire competitive landscape, we evaluated various applications and divided them into two major categories:
‍
INDIRECT COMPETITORS:
Marketplace - Platforms that allows recruiter to find qualified candidates.
Staffing Agency - Private agencies that are contracted to hire people.
Various Integration - 3rd party apps that could be integrated with other ATS.
Sourcing - Programmatic job distribution platforms.

DIRECT COMPETITORS
Applicant Tracking System - Hiring Automation Softwares.

Insight: where we positioned ourself in the sourcing vertical

After viewing the entire competitive landscape for sourcing, we have decided to target the “harmless weed” area as our MVP goal considering that other areas have very mature and well-established products/services that are dominating the business and we think this positioning strategy will yield the most value given the circumstances. As we evolve, we would like to slowly hatch onto other areas in the near future.

AI-driven services

AI driven services are being adopted by many ATS  to reduce manual labor. For example, BreezyHR uses AI chatbot to help recruiter to communicate with applicants,  which significantly reduce the workload of recruiters who need to handle mass communication.

Sourcing as a service

Sourcing service has been a hot cake in the ATS industry as many of the Fortune 1000 companies have sourcing needs. If we could nail sourcing service, it will allow us to sign more deals with these companies.

The sunset of staffing agency era

Some of the staffing agency that were popular are losing its competitive edge as more and more companies are establishing a dedicated hiring department within their own organization.

DISCOVERY - SURVEY

User expectations evolved overtime

Product team and marketing team collaborated on drafting an user survey that aims to validate some of assumptions and also measure our users' expectation. We sent out a survey to 2000 active accounts(account that has logged in at least once in the past 30 days) and received around 140 valid responses. some of the overarching survey questions are:

1. Who are our users? (age, gender, title, job responsibility etc)
2. What existing features better assist users' everyday tasks?
3. What features are not meeting users' expectation?
3. What feature do they want us to develop in the near future?

DISCOVERY - INTERVIEW

Small things sometimes cause big frustration when operating at scale

The survey results really shed light on some of our assumptions. However, in order to dive deeply into the underlying reasons, we have talked to some of domain experts and users to connect with them on a more personal level, which not only provides us with more contextual information but also allows us to stay empathetic with their struggles. through the user interviews, we want to get more clarity on the following issues

1. What are user’s motivation, goals, frustration and behavior patterns?
2. How do they work within their own team?
3. What is their everyday workflow?
4. How fountain could further assist them?

Small things matter

There are a lot of repetition in recruiter’s everyday task, even small product issues could cause a lot of frustration when it happens at a very large scale.

Operation manager needs more tools

Operation managers need better tools to optimize job funnels. For instance, they need A/B testing tool to test funnel performance and team collaboration tool to assign tasks.

Hourly workforce doesn’t have much patience

Workers need immediate gratification and confirmation, otherwise they would ghost you.

DESIGN - DESIGN PROCESS

Start small, move fast and iterate often

One good thing about working at a start up is that everything is very fast paced, the time that it takes from concept to launch is usually very short. We usually do one week design sprint to iterate ideas and get feedback. This process allows us to move at a very fast speed with minimal friction and cost, which i think has more pros than cons because speed sometimes is directly tied to success for start up.

Ideation

In the beginning of the design sprint, we start with breaking down the problem and providing arbitrary ideas, during this time, any idea is appreciated and is free from any backlash. After each design sprint, a group of people would get together to share what they have learned in the last sprint and reflect on what worked and what didn't.

Wireframe for stakeholder validation

Wireframe is often used to test ideas and quickly get feedback from stakeholders. Around this time, any feedback is welcome and it allows us to see how other people’s mental model works. We usually conduct poll to determine popular ideas.

Technical Specs

Once we have finalized and validated the wireframe, PM would start drafting the technical specs, which helps engineers understand the general scope and requirement of the project. The technical specs will get circulated across the company to get agreement on MVP scope and goals.

Design Iterations

This usually happens in parallel with the technical specs. The mid to high fidelity iteration usually focuses on the general usability, Interaction and visual presentation.

Usability Testing

Testing happens almost at every step of the design sprint, we test pretty mush whenever we think is necessary, sometimes it is only among internal stakeholder, but more often it is with the actual clients.

Many Iterations Later

Design detail : redesign status to improve screening efficiency

As one of the key components of the screening experience, status is probably the most challenging part of this redesign. Our goal is to have a lean status system that is easy to comprehend and pleasing to view. The challenge is that there are 13 different stage types and more than 80 different statuses.

Categorization

We mapped out the entire status label system and categorize them based on different stage types, triggers, and backend mechanism.

Heuristic Evaluation

We did a heuristic evaluation for the original status design and identified some obvious issues.

Design Iterations

Visually, we tried many alternatives to present the status and validated it among our regular users for intuitiveness check.

Regrouping

We brainstormed various ways to regroup all the statuses based on its types and use cases, we started with 4 groups and then slowly iterated to 6 groups

DESIGN - OPERATION EFFICIENCY

Empower the operation managers

Operation manager are the people that set up the funnels, oversee their performance and make global adjustment. They are like "the glue of the hiring process" and are responsible for the entire hiring cycle. However, there are not many tools dedicated to facilitate their everyday tasks. To fully understand their painpoints, we dived deeply into their workflow and identified some common issues.

Team Collaboration

The hiring team usually screen a very large scale of applicants on a daily basis, operation manager is responsible for assigning applicants to recruiters in order to balance workload within the team.

Make adjustment on a large scale

Operation manager carefully oversee the performance of all the funnels and make adjustment constantly, which is a very time consuming and repetitive process.

Convoluted workflow set up process

Not all operation manager are domain knowledge masters, workflow editor sometimes could be intimidating for beginners and usually require customer support team's assistance.

TASK CENTER

Operation managers now could view and filter all applicant-related tasks on this screen and assign them to each individual recruiter. This has hugely improved the team collaboration experience.

BULK EDITOR

Operation manager usually manage hundreds of funnels. bulk editor allows them to mass edit funnel to optimize its performance, which is a big step forward toward operation efficiency.

DESIGN - WORKFLOW SETUP

Conceptual Design - Reimagine the workflow setup process to enhance the onboarding experience and lower the operation cost.

The workflow setup process is considered the least user friendly aspect of the product as one needs to have a good amount of domain knowledge and best practices to make sense of the set up process, the setup process is the first major task that operation manager need to accomplish before the funnel could get up and run and is largely considered as a big part of the onboarding experience. We decided to optimize this process to allow new users to have a smoother on-boarding experience and lower the risk of losing customer in the beginning.

Outdated UI/UX

Through observing how new customers are being on-boarded, some of the confusion are caused by pure UI/UX problems.

Convoluted Language

The language used on workflow editor require some domain knowledge and often throw new users off.

High Operation Cost

Even though we have help center to onboard new users, on-screen instruction are missing from the current design. when users get stuck, contacting customer support is the only way to go, however, it usually resulted in high operation cost.

DESIGN - SOURCING

Improve the sourcing service

Sourcing service has been given a lot of strategic attention mainly for two reasons: first it is a relatively new service that we recently built and brought to the market in Q4 2018 and we need to nurture it. Second, it is projected to become the next major growth opportunity for the company . however, the previous MVP sourcing product is only a functional prototype instead of a fully fleshed product.  With that being said, there are a lot that we could do to improve it. We need to ace our game in order to hit the target revenue projected for the sourcing service. This project took me around 4 months to complete as we spent large amount of time on understand our users and scoping the solution. Luckily we reached a sweet spot and managed to increase the sourcing revenue by 125% compared to the previous version.

Only serve basic sourcing needs

The V1 build is only used by SMB clients and is largely considered to be too basic for enterprise clients.

Lack of data points

There is no analytics whatsoever to monitor the sourcing performance.

No campaign level sourcing

Enterprise users source for applicant on a very large scale but the previous version only support one sourced job at a time.

Design Detail: Sourcing Flow

The improved sourcing service has drastically improved the robustness of the sourcing experience, now users could customize it in a very detailed way to taylor toward their own sourcing needs. The customization includes:
1. Budget distribution
2. Job Posting Detail
3. Schedule and duration
4. Billing cycle
5. Coupon

SOURCING HISTORY

User could view the entire sourcing history and stop, suspend or re-source jobs. This allows user to manage sourced jobs on a large scale and significantly reduced their manual effort.

SOURCING REPORT PAGE

User could also look at the individual sourced job and see how the job is performing, it shows hoe the job is being distributed on job boards and the average cost for sourcing each applicant.

Monthly sourcing revenue increased by 125%

After being launched, we have closely monitor its adoption rate, according to sale team's statistics, the new revamped sourcing service has outperformed the older version by 125% in terms of revenue.

DESIGN - WORKER APP

Re-engage the workers

There are more than 3.5 Million workers that are screened on our platform every month, some of the workers ended up not getting any job offers and quit using our product, These workers are considered valuable assets from a strategic point of view and are the foundation of building an applicant marketplace,  in order to re-engage these workers, we have developed a native mobile app for workers to easily get job recommendations, apply for jobs and manage their application.

Design Detail: Application Flow Optimization

Majority of the workers don’t have much patience when applying for jobs. A large number of workers drop off from the application process because of the long application time. We spent a considerable amount of time optimizing the application process as well as the on-boarding experience to avoid midway applicant drop-offs.

DESIGN - WORKER PROFILE

Use gamification and endorsement to engage workers

We learned that frequent interaction among workers significantly lower the chance of user turning cold. In order to build a sense of community. we have designed a referral system and an endorsement system. The referral system uses gamification to reward users that refer their friends, in return, workers would get if they could successfully refer their friends to the job. The badges could later be converted to an internal scoring algorithm used for better discoverability on applicant marketplace.

The endorsement mechanism allows workers to send a endorsement request to their friends or employers for confirmation. Then the requested person could chose from a variety of traits and skill labels to describe the workers.

DESIGN - DESIGN SYSTEM

Building the design system from ground up

The design system has been happening in parallel with many other projects, we spent a lot of time studying the best practice from other mature design system (Google, IBM, Airbnb etc). The design system has been iterated for N+ rounds to accommodate the requirement of each team and declutter all the components used in our product, while staying lean and visually consistent. The design system has significantly reduced our iteration time as we could easily grab a components and apply it to different flows.
