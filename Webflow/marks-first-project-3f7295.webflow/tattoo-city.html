Overview


Tattoo city is a mobile app that aims to enable tattoo lovers and tattoo artists to express their feeling, stay connected and get inspired.  This is an ambitious project that tries to unify the tattoo industry by not only offering social media features but also incorporating E-commerce and AR feature,  at the same time providing a enjoyable and interesting experience for general public.

Word Counts: 1200
Estimated Time for Reading: 15 minutes

Team

Individual Project

My Role

Conducting user research, prototype and testing

Introduction

Building a centralized platform for tattoo lovers and tattoo business owner.

It is more and more often that we see people with tattoos on the street, TV and social media platforms, Tattoos is used be be generally considered as a rebellious symbol, However, over the years, it has been considered to be a form of art, a way to show a person’s individuality and personality.  With more and more people getting tattoo, the community is growing larger than ever.

We want to build an online community/platform to
‍
- Help general public better appreciate tattoo as an art form
- Help people get tattoos that suit their personality and taste
- Connect studios, artists and tattoo lovers
- Help artist, studio better showcase their work
- Boost the business for both studio and individual artist
- Help people show their tattoos to the world

An ambitious idea to resolve stigma around tattoo.

Promote tattoos, artists and studios with love

Quantitive Research

Understand the target demographic characteristics, general attitude and behavior.

We distributed the survey on mechanical turks and received 127 responses, this quantitive research helped us better understand the general demographic information, which helped us further categorize users into different types.

Qualitative Research

Combined with user in interviews, we have broken the users into three types

We have conducted 8 semi-structured user interviews covering a wide range of people who holds either positive or negative ideas about tattoo, the research is structured to understand user’s needs in a more empathetic manner. Combined with our statistical result, We have broken the users into three types and we have summarized some of the shared traits for each type of user.

Type 1.  Tattoo Enthusiasts

- Usually have three or more tattoos.
- Have certain taste and aesthetics about tattoos and tattoo artists.
- Usually have a few favorite artists and have expert knowledge about certain genre of tattoos.
- Very likely to have more tattoos in the futures.
- Constantly browse tattoo related content online, or on social media.
- Some use dedicated apps or forums to keep up with the trend.

Type 2.  Tattoo Lovers

- Have only one or two tattoo usually.
- They usually had the first tattoo for a special person, occasion, symbol, memory.
- Some are hesitant about getting another tattoo unless it is for good reason(good design, people, memory etc), some would not have any more tattoos.
- Don’t have extensive knowledge about tattoo genre and artists would like to be more educated in term of tattoos and artist.
- Tend to only use social media to occasionally find tattoo-related content.

Type 3.  Tattoo Novice

- Don’t have any tattoos.
- Generally feel positive about tattoo and the idea of getting a tattoo.
- Thought about getting a tattoo at one point in life but ended up not doing so for various reasons.
- Fear of pain and fear of regret are two common reason that hold them back from getting tattoos.
- Tend not to intentionally browse related content but would linger if stumbled upon interesting. tattoo related content on the internet or social media.

Qualitative Research

Conducted contextual inquiries among professional tattoo artists and business owners.

Furthermore, we have conducted 4 contextual inquiries across 3 tattoo shops to get to understand their work environment and their needs.
‍
Artists
- Most of them are affiliated and some own their personal studio.
- Specialized in certain genre and has his or her own style.
- Most of them have their own online portfolio website.
- Sometimes do commission-based tattoo proposal/sketch.
- Regularly post work on social media and have followers.
- Feel frustrated when consumer ask them to imitate other artist’s style.
- Like to get more exposed and recognized in the industry.  

Business Owners
- Have multiple artists that have distinct styles that could meet the needs of most customers.
- Offer free consultation and discount for first time customer.
- Mostly do call-based reservation and in-person consultation.
- Generally have website to showcase their range of work.
- Active on social media and other platforms for publicity.

Key Findings

We have identified some interesting finding across board.

Through the interviews with both tattoo lovers and tattoo artist, we have summarized some key findings.

Hesitant about getting tattoos due to fear

People generally are hesitant about getting a tattoo due to fear of pain and some worried that it would not turn out ideal and they would regret later in life.

Can’t connect with tattoo artists

Most people don’t know how to connect with artist for more information or consultation as there are not many centralized channels that bridge the communication gap.

The industry is flooded with artists that is not qualified

Since it is a big industry, it is flooded with tattoo artists with different skill level, people don’t have an authoritative source to validate the qualification pf an artist.

General public associate tattoo with negative impression

There are still a considerable amount of people that still consider tattoo as a rebellious symbol due to lack of knowledge about tattoo being an art form.

Tattoo artists don’t get enough exposure

Most of the tattoo artists have their own online portfolio, but still don’t get any mainstream exposure and is often considered as  “underground hippies”  

Tattoo artists sometimes work for nothing in return

Sometimes people would requested artist to submit proposal and ended up not paying anything if they don’t like it, which puts artist time and effort in vain.

Use Scenario Storyboard

Different user groups have relatively distinct use scenarios.

We have generated several storyboards where we walked through some scenarios to understand how user’s need varies based on spatial and temporal changes. Taking tattoo novice people for instance, as depicted in the storyboard, they get intrigued by seeing beautiful tattoos, but their attempt of getting a tattoo often got held back by not being able to predict how it might turn out in the end.
